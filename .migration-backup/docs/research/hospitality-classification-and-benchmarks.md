## Property Descriptors for Comp Search

### Tier 1 — Absolute Minimum (8 fields)
1. Location (city/submarket or GPS)
2. Country
3. Chain scale / quality tier
4. Service level (full/select/limited/extended/all-inclusive)
5. Number of rooms
6. Brand affiliation (or independent)
7. Year built
8. Last major renovation year

### Tier 2 — Required for Financial Modeling (7 more)
9. Location type (urban/suburban/resort/airport/highway)
10. Market tier (gateway/secondary/tertiary)
11. Guest mix (% business / leisure / group)
12. F&B presence (full restaurant / breakfast only / none / all-inclusive)
13. Meeting/event space (yes/no, approx sq ft)
14. Management type (brand-managed / third-party / owner-operated)
15. Franchise/brand agreement details

### Tier 3 — Enhances Quality (10 more)
16-25: Room type mix, trailing ADR/occ, comp set, demand generators, parking, spa/wellness, F&B revenue share, currency, labor context

## Occupancy Ramp-Up (% of Stabilized Occupancy)

| Year | Limited-Service | Select-Service | Full-Service | Luxury |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | 65-75% | 60-70% | 55-65% | 50-60% |
| Year 2 | 85-90% | 80-90% | 75-85% | 70-80% |
| Year 3 | 95-100% | 90-100% | 85-95% | 80-90% |
| Year 4 | 100% | 100% | 95-100% | 90-95% |
| Year 5 | Stabilized | Stabilized | 100% | 95-100% |

### ADR During Ramp-Up
- Year 1: 85-95% of stabilized ADR
- Year 2: 90-98%
- Year 3: 95-100%
- After stabilization: inflation + 0-1% annually

### Conservative vs Aggressive (Investor Lens)
**Conservative:** 4-5 year ramp, Year 1 at 55% of stabilized, no ADR premium
**Moderate:** 3-4 year ramp, Year 1 at 60-65%, modest Year 3 ADR premium
**Aggressive (red flag):** 2 year ramp, Year 1 at 70%+, immediate ADR premium
