# Similar Hospitality Business Models — Research Summary

## Most Relevant Comparables

### Ennismore (THE aspirational model)
- Multi-brand lifestyle platform: Hoxton, Mama Shelter, 25hours, SLS, Mondrian
- Merged with Accor 2021 (~66% Accor / 34% Ennismore). Accor provides distribution (90M+ loyalty members), Ennismore provides creative DNA
- F&B at 30-50% of revenue. Mama Shelter close to 50/50. Restaurants are standalone destinations, not hotel amenities.
- Dis-loyalty program: rewards trying different brands, community-first
- Fees: 2-3% base + 8-10% incentive + 1-2% brand + 1-2% marketing
- ~100+ properties, 100+ pipeline

### Nobu Hospitality (restaurant-first hotel brand)
- F&B at 40-60% of revenue — proves 50/50 is achievable
- Restaurant brand preceded hotel brand (40+ years of Nobu restaurants)
- Fees: 4-6% license/brand (higher than typical, reflecting restaurant brand premium) + 2-3% management
- ~15-20 properties, 20-30+ pipeline
- Key insight: F&B must be a destination that draws non-hotel guests

### Auberge Resorts (conversion specialist)
- Converts estates, ranches, historic properties into luxury resorts
- Examples: Hacienda AltaGracia (coffee estate), Commodore Perry Estate (Austin), Wildflower Farms (Hudson Valley)
- Fees: 3-4% base + 10-15% incentive + 1-2% brand + pre-opening fees ($1-3M+)
- ~20-25 properties. Backed by Friedkin Group (patient family office capital)
- Grows deliberately: 2-4 properties/year

### Grupo Habita (closest LatAm analog)
- Converts unique existing buildings in Mexico into boutique hotels
- Each property unique — no cookie-cutter. Deep cultural integration.
- ~10-15 properties over 20+ years. Deliberate, slow growth.
- Validates the conversion model in Latin America

### Selina (LatAm cautionary tale)
- WHAT WENT WRONG: Master leases (fixed costs regardless of occupancy), grew to 100+ properties before proving unit economics, low RevPAR ($30-80/night), multi-country complexity exploded, went public via SPAC at $1.2B, collapsed, filed for bankruptcy
- LESSONS: Don't use leases (use management contracts), prove at 5-10 before scaling past 20, premium positioning more forgiving than budget, multi-country LatAm is extraordinarily complex

## Scaling Phases

| Phase | Properties | Corporate Team | Overhead | Fee Income | Margin |
|-------|-----------|---------------|----------|------------|--------|
| Prove (1-5) | 3-5 years | 10-20 | $2-4M | $2-4M | Breakeven |
| Build (5-15) | 3-5 years | 30-60 | $5-10M | $7-12M | 10-20% |
| Accelerate (15-30) | 3-5 years | 60-100 | $10-18M | $15-25M | 20-35% |
| Platform (30-50+) | Ongoing | 80-150 | $15-25M | $25-50M | 30-45% |

**Breakeven: 8-15 managed properties, 3-7 years from founding**

## Management Company Valuations

| Stage | Revenue Multiple | EBITDA Multiple |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Early (5-10 properties) | 2-5x fee revenue | 8-12x EBITDA |
| Growth (15-30) | 6-10x | 12-18x |
| Mature (30+) | 10-15x | 18-25x |
| Premium with franchise | 15-20x+ | — |

**Brand/franchise income valued higher than management fee income** (more scalable, higher margin, more predictable).

## Key Strategic Recommendations
1. Management contracts, NOT leases (Selina lesson)
2. Prove unit economics at 3-5 properties before accelerating
3. Invest in F&B operating expertise — consider chef/restaurant partnerships
4. Build technology from day one (Life House thesis)
5. Geographic clustering: 2-3 markets initially
6. Separate management company from property ownership (different investor bases, higher management co. valuations)
7. Plan for 5-7 years to management company profitability
8. The Ennismore playbook is the aspiration: build brands → management contracts → strategic partnership for scale
