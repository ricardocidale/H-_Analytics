## Critical Risks the Model Must Capture

1. **Utah water rights** — Commercial use requires different water rights than residential. Scarce. Can be a dealbreaker.
2. **Utah liquor license quota** — Limited statewide supply, cannot be transferred. Must secure before/during development. Hotel license requires 40+ rooms (may not apply to 20-room boutique). Bar licenses cost $50K-500K+ on secondary market.
3. **NY SEQRA review** — Can add 3-12 months to timeline
4. **NY SLA 500-foot rule** — Neighbors can protest liquor licenses
5. **Septic capacity** — Often the binding constraint on rural properties for room count expansion
6. **Cartagena heritage zones** — Ministry of Culture approval required. 24-36+ month timelines.
7. **Properties under 12-15 rooms** struggle to cover fixed operating costs
8. **Corridor width** — Residential 36" hallways don't meet commercial 44" minimum. Common deal-breaker in mansion conversions.
9. **Elevator** — Required for 3+ stories or 3,000+ SF/floor. $75K-200K. Often the single most expensive ADA item.

## Timeline: Acquisition to Opening
- US: 18-36 months (24 typical)

## Successful Conversion Examples
- **Troutbeck (Amenia, NY):** 250-acre estate → 37 rooms. ~$500-800K/key. ADR $400-800+.
- **Hasbrouck House (Stone Ridge, NY):** 50-acre, 1757 house → 19 rooms across multiple buildings. ~$260-420K/key. ADR $200-400.
- **Inness (Accord, NY):** 225-acre dairy farm → 28 cabins. ADR $400-700+. Golf + farm-to-table programming.
- **Casa San Agustín (Cartagena):** Heritage houses → 31-room luxury. ADR $400-800+. Heritage restrictions made it painful.
- **Camp Sarika at Amangiri (Utah):** Tented pavilions at $3K-8K/night. Proves Utah luxury glamping works.

## Key Lesson
A-frame/cabin additions on acreage are the most capital-efficient way to expand room count. Main house becomes common areas (lobby, restaurant, bar, event space) with rooms in additions and outbuildings. This multi-building estate format allows phased development.
