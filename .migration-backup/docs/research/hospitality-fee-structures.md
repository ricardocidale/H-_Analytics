# Hospitality Management Fee Structures — Research Summary

## Base Management Fee
- **Boutique operators:** 2%–5% of gross revenue (3%–4% most common)
- **Major flags (Marriott, Hilton):** 2%–4% of gross revenue (3% standard)
- **Revenue base:** Total gross revenue (rooms + F&B + other operated departments)
- **Ramp-up provisions:** Common to start at 1.5%–2% in years 1–2, full rate by year 3

## Incentive Management Fee
- **Boutique operators:** 5%–10% of Adjusted GOP
- **Major flags:** 8%–12% of Adjusted GOP
- **Owner's priority return hurdle:** 8%–12% of owner's equity (more common with boutique operators)
- **Subordination:** Boutique operators more likely to accept fee subordination to debt service

## Brand/Franchise Fees (Boutique vs Major Flag)

### Major Flag (Marriott, Hilton, etc.)
| Component | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Royalty/franchise fee | 4%–6% of rooms revenue |
| Marketing/brand fund | 1.5%–3% of rooms revenue |
| Loyalty program | 4%–6% of rooms revenue |
| Reservation/distribution | 1%–2% of rooms revenue |
| **Total** | **10%–15% of rooms revenue** |

### Boutique/Soft Brand Operator
| Component | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Brand fee/licensing | 1%–3% of total revenue |
| Marketing fund | 1%–2% of total revenue |
| Reservation/technology | 0.5%–1.5% of total revenue |
| Loyalty (if any) | 0%–1% of total revenue |
| **Total** | **3%–7% of total revenue** |

## Centralized Service Fee Breakdown (% of total revenue)
| Service | Range |
|---------|-------|
| Technology & PMS/POS | 0.5%–1.5% |
| Revenue management | 0.3%–0.8% |
| Centralized reservations | 0.5%–1.5% |
| Accounting & financial reporting | 0.5%–1.0% |
| Marketing & brand | 1.0%–2.5% |
| Procurement/supply chain | 0.2%–0.5% |
| HR/training | 0.2%–0.5% |
| **Total** | **3%–7%** |

## P&L Placement (USALI Standard)
- Management fees (base + incentive) go **below the GOP line**
- Brand/franchise fees for major flags go in undistributed expenses (above GOP)
- Boutique operators who bundle fees — placement varies

## International Variations
- **Colombia:** 2%–4% base, 8%–10% incentive. Owner's priority returns commonly accepted. Fees sometimes USD-denominated. 10%–15% withholding on cross-border payments. 19% IVA.
- **France:** 2%–3% base, 6%–10% incentive. Lease model (bail commercial) common — changes entire P&L structure.
- **Spain:** 2%–3.5% base, 8%–10% incentive. Seasonal properties need fee minimums. 21% IVA.
- **Mexico:** 2%–4% base, 8%–10% incentive. All-inclusive resorts change revenue base calculation.
- **Brazil:** 3%–5% base, 10%–15% incentive. Condo-hotel model common. BRL-denominated with IPCA adjustments.

## Investor Red Flags
- No incentive fee (operator paid regardless of performance)
- Incentive fee from first dollar (no owner's priority)
- No subordination clauses
- No performance termination right
- Total fee load > 12%–15% of revenue
- Operator retains procurement rebates
- Bundled fees with no breakdown

## Investor Green Flags
- Incentive fee tied to GOP/NOI performance
- Owner's priority return before incentive fee
- Fee subordination to debt service
- Transparent service fee breakdowns
- Performance test after year 2–3
- Total fee load under 8%–10% of revenue
