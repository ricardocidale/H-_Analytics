# Vertical/Community-Focused Boutique Hospitality — Market Research

## Market Sizes
- **Wellness tourism:** ~$817B (2022) → $1.4T by 2027 (12-13% CAGR). Wellness tourists spend 53% more per trip.
- **Corporate retreats:** $50-70B, growing 15-20% CAGR post-COVID. 65-75% of remote-first companies hold 2+ offsites/year.
- **Sexual wellness:** Broader market $80-100B. Retreat segment small but fast-growing, driven by destigmatization.

## The Quality Gap — CONFIRMED
Most small vertical-focused hotels charge premium prices but deliver mediocre experiences:
- Retreat centers run by practitioners (yoga teachers, therapists) who know programming but not hotel operations
- No revenue management, F&B as afterthought, deferred maintenance
- "Paying Four Seasons prices for Holiday Inn rooms"
- Hedonism II: $300-500/night, 3.5-star physical product, survives on monopoly position
- **Clear gap for branded, quality-controlled, 10-30 room intimate properties**

## Competitor Landscape

### Wellness
- Canyon Ranch, Miraval: 100+ rooms, institutional, aging demographic
- Six Senses (LVMH): closest comp to "brand of boutique wellness hotels"
- Gap: nothing branded at 10-30 room estate scale

### Sexual Wellness
- Desire Resorts (Mexico): market leader but lifestyle/swinger-coded
- Hedonism II: dated, mediocre quality
- Most tantra/intimacy retreats rent venues, don't own them
- **No branded, high-quality boutique hotel for sexual wellness education that is NOT swinger-coded**

### Corporate Retreats
- Choice is: generic hotel conference center OR rent an Airbnb estate with no services
- **No branded network of estate properties with built-in retreat programming and F&B**

## ICP Profiles

### Wellness Guest
- 35-60yo, 70% female, HHI $150K+, urban
- Spends $2K-5K per retreat (3-5 days)
- 30-50% repeat rate if experience excellent
- Trigger: burnout, life transition, health wake-up call

### Sexual Wellness Guest
- 30-55yo, couples 70% / singles 30%, HHI $120K+, progressive
- Spends $2.5K-6K per couple for weekend
- Privacy concern: HIGH — won't post on social media
- Practitioner referral is the primary acquisition channel

### Corporate Retreat Booker
- EA/Chief of Staff/People Ops (80% female, 28-45)
- Companies: tech, professional services, 20-200 employees
- Budget: $1.5K-3.5K per person for 2-3 day offsite
- Win the planner = recurring 3-4x/year + referrals

## Pricing Ranges

### Wellness (3-day)
- Mid-range: $1,500-3,000/person
- Premium: $3,000-6,000/person

### Sexual Wellness (weekend)
- Mid-range with accommodation: $1,500-3,000/person
- Premium couples: $3,000-6,000/couple

### Corporate (2-3 days)
- Mid-range: $1,500-2,500/person
- Premium: $2,500-4,000/person

### US vs Colombia
- Colombia pricing 40-60% lower for comparable quality
- $2K retreat in Colombia = $5K+ product in US
- Cost structure allows much higher quality at accessible prices

## F&B at 50/50 — Achievable

### How (for 20-room estate):
- 20 rooms × 75% occ × $350 ADR = ~$1.9M rooms revenue
- Need ~$1.9M F&B = ~$5,200/day across all outlets
- In-house guests (all-inclusive): $100-150/person × 30 guests = $3K-4.5K/day
- Bar revenue: $500-1K/day
- Events/private dining: $500-1.5K/day average
- **Tight but achievable** with event programming and all-inclusive packaging

### Ennismore examples:
- The Hoxton: ~40-45% F&B revenue
- Mama Shelter: close to 50/50
- Key: F&B must attract non-hotel guests (locals, walk-ins)

## Community/Membership — The Moat
- Soho House: $3.6K-5.5K/year, 200K+ members, ~40% of revenue from dues
- For wellness: $500-2K/year membership with priority booking, virtual programming, exclusive weekends
- For sexual wellness: higher willingness to pay ($1K-3K/year) because trust and safety are paramount
- For corporate: B2B membership $5K-20K/year with dedicated planning support
- **The practitioner partnership model solves marketing** — practitioners bring audiences, brand provides venue + operational excellence

## Key Risk Identified
**Scheduling conflict:** Running multiple verticals from same properties requires careful segmentation. A corporate team and a tantra retreat cannot be in the same building at the same time. Need either dedicated properties per vertical OR very careful scheduling/calendar management.

## Regulatory — Sexual Wellness
- Education/workshops: no special licensing (protected speech)
- If facilitators provide therapy: must be licensed in the state
- Need supplemental insurance: professional liability, participant injury, sexual misconduct coverage
- Robust waivers essential
- Colombia: more permissive, but must position clearly as education
- Utah: restrictive liquor laws will affect F&B model
