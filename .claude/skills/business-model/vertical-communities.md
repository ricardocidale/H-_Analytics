# Vertical Communities

## The Opportunity
Most small vertical-focused hotels charge premium prices but deliver mediocre experiences. "Paying Four Seasons prices for Holiday Inn rooms." This brand delivers better quality at fair prices.

## Target Verticals
| Vertical | ICP | Spend/Trip | Repeat Rate | Key Channel |

## Practitioner-Led Marketing Model
- Facilitators (yoga teachers, tantra educators, coaches) BRING their audiences
- Brand provides venue + operational excellence
- A practitioner with 50K followers fills a 20-room retreat in one post
- Revenue split: typically 60-70% to property, 30-40% to facilitator
- The practitioner IS the marketing channel for wellness and sexual wellness

## Scheduling Conflict Rule
**A corporate team and a sexual wellness retreat CANNOT be at the same property simultaneously.**
- Either dedicate properties to verticals
- Or implement calendar-based vertical separation
- This reduces effective capacity — model must account for it

## Pricing (US vs Colombia)
Colombia pricing is 40-60% lower for comparable quality. A $2K retreat in Colombia delivers a $5K+ product.

## Regulatory — Sexual Wellness
- Education/workshops: no special licensing (protected speech)
- If therapy: practitioners must be licensed in the state
- Supplemental insurance needed: professional liability, participant injury, misconduct coverage
- Robust waivers essential
- Colombia: more permissive, position clearly as education

## Market Sizes
- Wellness tourism: ~$817B → $1.4T by 2027 (12-13% CAGR)
- Corporate retreats: $50-70B, growing 15-20% CAGR post-COVID
- Sexual wellness: $80-100B broader market, retreat segment emerging fast
