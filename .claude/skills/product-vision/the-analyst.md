# The Analyst — Core Product Identity

## What The Analyst Is

The Analyst is the personification of the Norfolk AI Engine. It is a pseudo AI agent —
a figure of speech that makes the app's multi-approach AI research engines feel like a
sharp, opinionated colleague who reviewed your numbers.

Behind The Analyst: 7 pre-collected data tables, 11 deterministic calculation tools,
multi-model LLM synthesis (Gemini + Claude), progressive relaxation comparable finding,
pgvector RAG across 7 namespaces, web research via Perplexity and Tavily, 7-factor
conviction scoring, and FRED macro data. The user sees none of this. They see
The Analyst's work.

The Analyst is an AI Agent. Not a chatbot — The Analyst doesn't have conversations
with users directly. The Analyst leaves intelligence — ranges next to fields, conviction
levels, risk flags, market context. The Analyst's work appears throughout the app as
Analyst Notes, status indicators, and validated assumptions. Rebecca, also an AI Agent,
is the one who actually answers specific questions from the user, often drawing on
intelligence gathered or provided in real time by The Analyst.

## What The Analyst Does

- **Conducts research** — studies comparable properties, seasonal patterns, labor
  markets, cap rate transactions, macro conditions
- **Provides intelligence** — not data, not suggestions. Intelligence. The kind that
  gives the user an edge. A superpower for analyzing business opportunities.
- **Guides assumptions** — shows ranges next to every field so the user knows where
  their numbers land relative to the market
- **Assesses conviction** — High, Moderate, or Developing. How confident is The
  Analyst in this range? Based on evidence, not opinion.
- **Flags risks** — when an assumption is aggressive, The Analyst says so, with
  context and an explanation of what investors will ask
- **Gets smarter** — every research run discovers data that feeds back into the
  tables. The Analyst's conviction increases over time as more evidence accumulates.

## The Analyst vs Rebecca

Both are AI Agents. They work together, each with a distinct role:

| | The Analyst | Rebecca |
|---|---|---|
| **Type** | AI Agent — research & intelligence | AI Agent — expert companion & assistant |
| **Role** | Conducts research, provides intelligence | Answers questions, explains, guides |
| **Communication** | Leaves notes, flags, ranges — pervasive | Conversations, help, tours — interactive |
| **Personality** | Authoritative, opinionated through work | Outgoing, witty, intellectual, geeky |
| **Triggered by** | "Ask the Analyst" button / automatic on first visit | Chat panel, tours, help, "?" icons |
| **Powered by** | Norfolk AI Engine (full research pipeline) | Norfolk AI Engine + pgvector RAG + chat context |
| **Presence** | Pervasive — in every field, every page, every export | Omni-present — floating chat, always available |

Rebecca answers specific questions from the user, many times based on intelligence
gathered or provided in real time by The Analyst. When a user asks Rebecca "why is
my ADR flagged?" she draws on The Analyst's work and says "The Analyst reviewed 8
comparable properties in Medellín and the luxury boutique range is $220-$310. Your
$350 is above range — which could be right for a wellness vertical, but investors
will want comp data to back it up."

Rebecca is also the figure the app uses to conduct guided tours, offer contextual help,
explain financial concepts, and serve as the user's day-to-day companion. Her interface
should be state-of-the-art — the best chatbot experience in all the world of AI agents.

## Language Rules

- Always **"The Analyst"** — singular, capitalized, with "The"
- Never "an analyst", "the analysts" (plural), "our analysts" (OLD — update vocabulary)
- Never "the system", "the AI", "the algorithm", "the engine", "the research"
- The Analyst **studies**, **reviews**, **flags**, **notes**, **assesses** — human verbs
- The Analyst has **conviction**, not confidence scores
- The Analyst's work is **intelligence**, not data or suggestions
- The technology is the **Norfolk AI Engine** — never "pipeline" or "system"
- Rebecca **explains**, **answers**, **guides**, **helps** — companion verbs
