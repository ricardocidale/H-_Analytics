what are the Authorized redirect URIs for this project?

I am creating a Client in Google Cloud

Got it! For setting up the Google Cloud OAuth Client, you'll need to add these as your Authorized redirect URIs:

For production:

https://h-analysis.com/api/auth/google/callback
For development (Replit):
You'll also want to add your Replit dev URL. It would follow this pattern:

https://<your-repl-slug>.<your-username>.repl.co/api/auth/google/callback
You can find your exact Replit dev URL in the webview panel when the app is running.

Make sure both are added in the Google Cloud Console under APIs & Credentials → OAuth 2.0 Client IDs → Authorized redirect URIs so that login works in both environments.

what is my username?

I don't have access to your Google Cloud or Replit account details, so I can't see your username. But you can find your Replit username by looking at the URL in your browser — it's typically in the format replit.com/@YourUsername. Your current Replit dev URL will also be visible in the webview panel when the app is running.

could the username be my email address?

In the context of Google Cloud OAuth, no — the "username" I was referring to is your Replit username, not your email. It's the short handle you chose when you created your Replit account (like @john or @mycompany).

You can spot it easily — just look at the URL bar in your browser right now. It'll be something like:

replit.com/@YourUsername/YourProjectName
That @YourUsername part is what you need. Your Replit dev URL for the redirect URI would then look something like:

https://YourProjectName.YourUsername.repl.co/api/auth/google/callback
whre do I find that?

The easiest way to find it: look at the browser address bar right now while you're in your Replit project. The URL will show something like:

https://replit.com/@YourUsername/YourProjectName
The part right after the @ symbol is your Replit username.
