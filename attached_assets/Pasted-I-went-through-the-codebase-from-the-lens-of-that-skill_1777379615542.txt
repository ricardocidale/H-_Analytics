I went through the codebase from the lens of that skill. Quick context: H+ Analytics already has a lot of the financial modeling the skill describes — calc/returns/ (DCF, IRR, MIRR, equity multiple, exit valuation, WACC), calc/financing/ (DSCR, debt yield, sizing, prepayment, sensitivity), calc/analysis/ (break-even, hold-vs-sell, stress-test, RevPAR index), calc/research/ (cap-rate valuation, ADR projection, occupancy ramp, debt capacity), plus pages like PropertyFinder, PropertyMarketResearch, FinancingAnalysis, SensitivityAnalysis, ComparisonView, and MapView.

So this isn't a "you're missing investment math" audit. It's narrower: the skill is built for residential buyers/SFR investors, but several of its framings translate directly to your hospitality SPV use case and aren't fully present today. Here's where I think the skill actually adds value:

Strong fits — likely worth building

Acquisition price-history timeline. The skill insists on reconstructing original-list → every reduction → delist/relist for any target. For your acquisition pipeline (PropertyFinder), this surfaces motivated sellers and gives negotiation leverage. Today the Finder shows current ask, not the cut-history pattern.

Breakeven-style framing on the Sensitivity page. You have a stress-test and a sensitivity grid, but the skill's specific framing — breakeven rent, rent for 8% CoC, breakeven purchase price at target cap, breakeven mortgage rate, appreciation needed to offset losses — is a different artifact than a stress matrix. The hospitality translation is breakeven ADR, breakeven occupancy, breakeven RevPAR, breakeven going-in cap, breakeven debt rate, breakeven terminal-cap movement. Currently the page shows scenario outputs but not the explicit "what does X need to be for this deal to work" reverse-solve.

Exit scenarios at 3/5/7/10 years across multiple appreciation rates with a clear breakeven hold period and "early-exit risk" flag. You have hold-vs-sell.ts and exit-valuation.ts, but the skill's table format (sale price → selling costs → loan balance → net proceeds → annualized ROI per horizon × pessimistic/base/optimistic) plus an explicit "minimum hold to break even after costs" line is a clearer investor-facing artifact than what PropertyDetail shows today.

"HOA deep dive" → FF&E reserve / brand-cost deep dive. The skill's reserve-fund + special-assessment + master-policy gap analysis maps cleanly to FF&E reserve adequacy (USALI 4% benchmark), brand PIP exposure, HMA/franchise fee stack, condo-hotel association dues, post-Surfside structural milestone risk for coastal FL properties. I didn't see a dedicated "reserve adequacy + assessment exposure" panel.

Submarket analysis equivalent of the neighborhood checklist. The skill's checklist (walk/transit, flood zone, future development, market velocity) translates to STR submarket comps, flight/airport capacity, event calendar, comp-hotel new-supply pipeline, zoning changes around the asset, short-term rental restriction trends, hurricane/flood zone insurance load. Some of this lives in market intelligence; the new-supply pipeline and STR restriction trend are the gaps I'd flag.

Own-vs-rent → Own-vs-lease-vs-manage decision. The own-vs-rent comparison structurally maps to buy fee-simple vs. master-lease vs. sign HMA vs. franchise for a given asset. This is a real strategic question for boutique portfolios and I don't see it modeled side-by-side.

Pre-offer due-diligence checklist tailored for hospitality. The skill's checklist is residential. The hospitality version (ALTA survey, Phase I environmental, structural, ADA audit, brand PIP scope, liquor license transferability, OTA contract assignability, lodging-tax compliance, union/WARN exposure, wind/flood policy) would be a useful artifact attached to each acquisition target.

Weak fits — skip

Mortgage affordability / DTI / 28-36 rule (institutional SPV buyer, not a household).
1% rule / 50% rule / 70% rule (residential heuristics; you already use cap rate, debt yield, DSCR, EBITDA multiples).
GreatSchools/crime data (low signal for boutique hotel demand).
