For real estate and hospitality market research, the “best” LLM setup is usually a combination of a top‑tier general model plus domain data, not a single standalone model.

Which base models to use
For 2026, independent comparisons show that frontier models like GPT‑5/4.1‑class, Claude 3.7/4‑class, and Gemini 2‑class sit at the top of reasoning and analysis benchmarks. In practice, any of these can work well if you wrap them with the right prompts, tools (RAG, code, SQL), and data sources.

A good pattern for your use case is:

One frontier model for deep reasoning and report‑style synthesis (e.g., GPT‑class, Claude‑class, or Gemini‑class).

One cheaper or open‑source model (Llama 4, Mistral, Qwen, Gemma, Phi) for bulk tasks, log parsing, and quick iterations.

Why domain data matters more
Real estate and hospitality use cases depend heavily on local, time‑sensitive data (comps, STR/CoStar data, zoning, permitting, pipeline, tourism stats), which no base LLM “knows” reliably out of the box. The serious tools in this space all combine LLMs with:

Retrieval‑augmented generation over proprietary data (market reports, leases, permits, zoning, AVM outputs).

Traditional ML/AVM engines (CoreLogic/“Cotality,” Quantarium, HouseCanary, etc.) that feed valuations and risk measures into the LLM for explanation and scenario building.
